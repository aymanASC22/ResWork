Resume Writing 101

MAIN POINT:

  • Your resume should stand out, but also be clear and concise.

FORMATTING

  • The FIRST THING on your resume should be your basic information, i.e. your name, number, email, and Linkedin or GitHub is applicable.
  • The top third of your resume should be utilized the most! Employers will not want to read the rest of your resume if it doesn't pull them in at first.
  • An objective can be added in this area as well if you are experiencing a career change.
  • More valuable than an objective, however, is a "snapshot." Essentially, you can try to capture yourself and what you can offer to the prospective employer if you are hired.

    Experience:

  • The next section of your resume should detail your experience.
  • This includes, but is not limited to, previous employment, internships, volunteer work, and other opportunities.
  • Try to emphasize what is most applicable to the position you are applying for.

    Education

  • The education section should have a list of all completed school levels starting from high school.
  • The list should go from highest level of education, to lowest level.

    Skills & Abilities

  • Include a list of any skills that not only show your strengths for the position, but skills that also make you stand out from the pool of applicants.
  • This can span from languages you speak, to softwares you are efficient in (such as coding languages or Microsoft Office), to any extra credentials you have.

STYLISTIC CHOICES

  • It may seem trivial, but stylistic choices for your resume go a long way.
  • You should always keep in mind that you want to present a professional image of yourself. This is done with a professional font, using academic language instead of slang, and clear organization.

GENERAL ADVICE

  • Make sure your information is relevant to the position you want!
  • Whatever is more relevant should be listed higher on your resume.
  • Use a lot of keywords, such as action verbs, and a lot of numbers and statistics!
  • Many companies use computer programs to get through many applications at once, therefore not only is using keywords important, but so is making your resume easy to read!
  • Avoid cluttering your resume with unnecessary words.
  • Make sure everything is as brief as possible without taking away the essence of what you want to show.
  • Only use sections that you need! The template is not a definitive outline. If you believe that a certain section does not apply to you or is not necessary, do not be afraid to leave it out!
  • Make sure to proofread your resume for spelling and grammatical errors! It is not a good look to have simple mistakes.
  • Overall, do not think you have to use the same resume for every application. It is okay to tweak certain things to make it more applicable for the position you want.
