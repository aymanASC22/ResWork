lesson 2: Interview

MAIN POINT:

  • When you walk into the room for a interview you want to walk in standing up straight with a smile

how to prepare

  • Before beginning your interveiw you should do research on your job as well as learn and memorize your interveiwers name.
  • It is very importent to show up on time to your interveiw. It is recommended that you reach your interview destination 15 minutes before the interview begins. Not only is this common courtesy but it also shows that you are serious about the position.
  • bring mutiple copys of your resume with you(see resume lesson for resume assistance).
  • practice answers to common interveiw questions. for example “Tell me about yourself, and why are you interested in this role with our company?”
  • prepare examples of your past works. specifically you want ta gather examples relating to the job you are trying to aquire.
  • bring a pen and note pad with you. Although you definitly should not take notes during your interveiw afterwards you should write down everything you remember, including how well you think you did.
  • plan interveiw attire the night before. dress casual but still professional.

    How to start things off

  • greet your interveiwer with a handshake. by doing so you show you are professional and courteous
  • when the interveiw starts do not jump right into business, follow the interveiwers lead

    During the interveiw

  • Maintain eye contact. This helps to show you are alert and paying attention
  • Answer questions truthfully. When taking an interveiw the last thing you want to do is lie, especially about your qualifications
  • show positivity. If your happy they might be happier.
  • listen carfully to your interveiwer and focus. you want to make sure that you hear and understand what they are asking.
  • when answering a question try to reference an accomplishment. you should use every oppertunity you can to address the requirements listed in the job description
  • never speak negitively about others around you. loyaly is importent

    After the interveiw is completed

  • after the interveiw is over you can ask what to expect next. usually the response is an email but it will vary.
  • when in an interveiw wait for the interveiwer to bring up salary.
  • no matter how the interveiw went close on a positive note.
  • even after the the interveiw you should send a not or letter statting your thanks for the opertunity
